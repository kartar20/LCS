How Much Litter Is There?

Litter is a big problem in schools and across the UK. Every day, more than two million pieces of litter are dropped in the UK. Also, plastic makes up over 70% of all litter, which is very harmful to the environment.

In schools, each student can create 20–45kg of waste every year, which adds up to millions of kilograms across the country. This shows that even small actions, like dropping wrappers, can become a huge problem.

At Littleover Community School, some benches are even made from recycled plastic, which proves how much waste is produced but also how it can be reused in a better way.

How Can We Stop Littering?

There are many simple ways to stop littering in our school. Firstly, students should always use bins and never drop rubbish on the ground. More bins around the school could also help, so people don’t have to walk far to throw things away.

Another idea is to have posters or assemblies to remind students why littering is bad. Recycling is also very important, because it can turn waste into useful things, like the plastic benches in school.

If everyone takes responsibility and works together, we can keep Littleover Community School clean and make a positive difference to the environment.
